# Ethics

## Purpose

Project NAVIDSON is designed to explore explainable behavioral anomaly detection using entirely synthetic organizational data.

The project exists as an educational and research platform for studying cybersecurity, machine learning, and human-centered decision support.

No real employee data, patient information, or proprietary organizational records are used.

---

# Design Philosophy

NAVIDSON is built on the principle that unusual behavior is not evidence of malicious intent.

Employee behavior changes for many legitimate reasons, including:

- Business travel
- Organizational restructuring
- Regulatory deadlines
- Software deployments
- Personal work habits
- Collaboration requirements
- Promotions and new responsibilities

Behavior should always be interpreted within its organizational context.

---

# Human-Centered Security

NAVIDSON is intended to support cybersecurity professionals—not replace them.

The system generates observations, confidence scores, and recommendations that assist human analysts in prioritizing investigations.

NAVIDSON is designed to support human judgment, not replace it. Final decisions should always remain the responsibility of qualified cybersecurity professionals.

---

# Explainability

Every recommendation produced by NAVIDSON should be explainable.

Analysts should be able to understand:

- Which behaviors changed
- How those behaviors differ from historical baselines
- Which organizational events influenced the assessment
- Why confidence increased or decreased

Explainability is treated as a core design requirement rather than an optional feature.

---

# Privacy by Design

Although Gladney Pharmaceuticals is fictional, NAVIDSON is designed around privacy-conscious principles.

The project avoids collecting or simulating unnecessary personal information.

Behavioral analysis focuses on work-related activity rather than personal identity or protected characteristics.

---

# Fairness

Employees are compared primarily against their own historical behavior.

Peer groups provide additional context but are not the primary basis for evaluation.

This approach reduces the likelihood of unfairly flagging employees whose work naturally differs from departmental averages.

---

# Confidence Accumulation

NAVIDSON avoids making decisions based on isolated events.

Instead, confidence develops gradually as multiple independent behavioral indicators align over time.

This approach helps reduce false positives while encouraging measured, evidence-based analysis.

---

# Intended Use

NAVIDSON is intended for:

- Education
- Academic research
- Software engineering
- Cybersecurity experimentation
- Explainable AI research
- Synthetic data generation research

The project is not intended for deployment in production environments without substantial validation, governance, and legal review.

---

# Limitations

NAVIDSON operates within a fictional organization using synthetic data.

Results produced by the system should not be interpreted as evidence of how real employees or organizations behave.

The simulation emphasizes realism, but it remains an educational model rather than a representation of any specific company.

---

# Ethical Principles

NAVIDSON is guided by the following principles:

- Context before judgment.
- Human oversight is essential.
- Confidence should accumulate gradually.
- Explainability builds trust.
- Privacy should be preserved whenever possible.
- Technology should assist—not replace—human decision making.
